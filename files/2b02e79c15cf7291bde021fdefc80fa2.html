Summary

“I've learned that making a 'living' is not the same thing as 'making a life'.” - Maya Angelou
"Don't confuse having a career with having a life." - Hillary Clinton

The two quotes are a great summary of what it means to be successful in life. Naquan is not in search of a full-time job; he is in search of a full-time career! Rising into his senior year at the Rochester Institute of Technology, Naquan King is entering the thick of the early stages of his career. His major- Information Technology, with minors in Business Administration and Communications. He spent the majority of his college career looking for ways to distinguish himself from being just the "IT Guy".

An aspiring Businessman, Naquan hopes to grow and develop transferable skills in Information Technology and Communication that will help him in his career explorations. Currently working as an intern in the Investment Banking Line of Business at J.P. Morgan Chase, Naquan is getting some great learning experience of the role of Technology in the Financial Services Industry.

Within the next few months, he hopes to learn more about specific areas of interest within the fields of Business and Technology. So just in case he's unsure about an exact career choice, he is aware of areas that he want to explore. This will guide him to strategically choosing a path that will lead to an enjoyable career and professional success.

What's next?
Looking down the line, Naquan hopes to return back to his school days to pursue a Graduate School education. Since many graduate school programs require a few years of industry experience, he will be looking to go to Grad school after completing at least two years of industry experience. But he's in no rush!

Most importantly, he values a positive, open work environment, stability and mobility to maneuver around within the company.

e-Business Model for Rowe Company based out of Rochester, NY

September 2012

This project proposed an e-Business model for a photography, video, and audio company based out of Rochester, NY. The current state of Rowe’s website is catalog based, where interested customer visit the website to view the selection of products, but are unable to make any purchases from Rowe’s website itself. The customer is directed to the manufacturer’s website for each product that is being offered. The team proposed an business model that would allow for customers to view and interact with the products on the website and make purchases directly from the website. The team made the redesigned the website to allow customers to have a more dynamic and efficient experience when browsing and shopping.
