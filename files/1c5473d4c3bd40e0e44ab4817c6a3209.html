Background

Summary

Bing Zhang is currently a transportation engineering analyst at the midtown Atlanta office of Kimley-Horn. She is a member of the transportation planning and traffic operations group. Miss Zhang has interned with Kimley-Horn at their headquarters in Cary, North Carolina for two summers. Bing is a registered Engineer-in-Training. She will be eligible to take the Professional Engineer licensure exam in April 2017.

Bing recently graduated from the Georgia Institute of Technology with a Masters of Science in Civil Engineering with a Transportation Engineering focus. Miss Zhang's research was in Social Networked Transportation which was supervised by Dr. Kari Watkins. Her Master's thesis is available at the Georgia Institute of Technology Library, and discusses the development of a social weights matrix to consider friendship influences on air travel behavior. Her paper has been accepted for presentation at the North America Regional Science Council conference in November 2014.

Miss Zhang received her Bachelors of Science in Civil Engineering degree from the University of Virginia where she obtained proficiency areas in Materials, Transportation, Structures, Infrastructure and Management, and Water Resources. Additionally, Bing minored in Materials Science and Engineering Business. Her two part undergraduate thesis portfolio in Queue Jump Lanes and Transit Equity Planning is available at the University of Virginia Library. Her research was supervised by Dr. Brian Park and Dr. Peter Norton.
