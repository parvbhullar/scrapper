Background

Summary

When I was eight years old, I told my mother I wanted to be a computer scientist when I grew up.

When I was in my late teens, becoming involved in online communities changed my life. I became invested in community building through forum management and moderation. The more I learned about online technologies, the more I became fascinated with the technical side of web applications. I am frequently amazed by the serendipitous turns my life takes through technology, and the amazing role that technology and innovation has in society and culture.

While deeply involved in online communities, I was surrounded by other technophiles, hackers, and programmers, many of whom remain close friends. It was through their guidance and encouragement that I decided to learn the craft of writing my own programs.

Most recently, I took the opportunity to attend MakerSquare, a full-time, immersive development bootcamp, to jumpstart my childhood ambition of writing software.

I'm excited about working in technology because I can create usable and compelling applications that make people's lives better and more connected.
