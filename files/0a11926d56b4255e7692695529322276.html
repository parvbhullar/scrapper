Background

Summary

My objective is to build intelligent machines to extend the capacity and capability of humanity. I believe this goal can best be reached by improving autonomous perception algorithms, identifying daily-life applications, and implementing learning systems to enable use by non-experts.

I have broad experience in computer science and artificial intelligence; my recent specialty is automated 3D perception and state estimation from fused sensor data (multiple views and/or sensing modalities), typically using machine learning. In a past life, I was more focused on decision theory: planning under uncertainty, multi-agent systems (humans, software AI, robots).

After five years of study towards my PhD, I took leave to start a spin-off company to take better advantage of my academic and professional groundwork. Then I got a job as a research/systems scientist at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, which is where I've stayed.

Note that I strongly prefer to build things in an applied research setting rather than write papers and give public talks; moreover, the information I deal with in my current job is highly sensitive and does not lend itself to publication. This balance is reflected in my work history and publications below.
