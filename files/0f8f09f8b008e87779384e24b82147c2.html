Background

Summary

I'm fascinated by the idea of collective intelligence and how it can be applied to decision-making. To this end I'm developing technologies to make sense of the vast quantities of consumer opinion expressed on the web. In the past I’ve worked on a variety of techniques for extracting structure from unstructured data through a judicious mix of computer and human intelligence.

Specialties: applied machine learning, text mining and analytics, algorithms, optimization, document analysis, sketch recognition, iterative prototyping, experimentation

Founded ChatterPop, a social media analytics company ahead of its time. Our software engine filtered massive amounts of product-related online chatter to summarize consumer opinion and make critical trends pop to attention. We focused on early adopter conversations in online forums, covered the launch of the first generation iPhone in mid-2007, and delivered concise automated summaries to analysts at regular intervals.

Research Scientist

Microsoft Research
February 2003 – November 2006 (3 years 10 months)

Statistical machine learning algorithms and their application to natural user interfaces. I focused on recognition and user interaction with digital ink, ranging from handwriting in unstructured ink notes and document annotations to spatially structured diagrams and equations. I helped architect and implement many aspects of ink recognition on the Tablet PC. My work was shipped as part of Tablet PC and Microsoft Office and was the basis for a 10-person development group in Belgrade. The work resulted in 18 patents pending/issued and 20 refereed publications at conferences such as ICCV, UIST, ICML, and CHI, most of which are available at http://shilman.net.

Mayfield Fellow

Presented client platform to several of the world’s largest wireless carriers as part of larger sales effort. Performed licensing due diligence for third-party Java Virtual Machines and WAP browsers.

Graduate Student Researcher

UC Berkeley
1996 – 2002 (6 years)

My dissertation, “Discerning Structure from Freeform Handwritten Ink” contributed three novel approaches for recognizing freeform handwritten notes, document annotations, and sketched diagrams. Before settling on sketch recognition, I worked on a wide variety of technical problems ranging from algorithm and information visualization in VLSI systems design, to embedded software methodologies, to user interfaces and recognition technologies for pen computers.

Software Engineer

The system has since become the industry standard, processing over $5B/US per day as the multi-site, nationwide solution for Bank of America, PNC, USBank, and Wells Fargo banks.
