Background

Summary

My background is in senior leadership and higher-risk operational roles in policing and I am now enjoying the successful transition I've made into the private sector where I am involved in ventures which merge the skills acquired in managing operational and strategic risk together with positive, practical methods to enable and support effective leadership.

I've led a number of multi-agency organisational change and modernisation projects, developing new ways of working, improving systems, processes and effectiveness using Prince2. I have led national training design programmes and established effective recruitment and selection processes, promoting equality and diversity in specialist roles. I was a police promotions assessor and chaired the national interview panel for selection of advanced level covert operatives. I have held national and international responsibilities as the chair of working groups concerned with strategy, operations and learning & development for covert officers in UK and foreign police forces.

I have designed testing and exercising events for private industry and for law enforcement, providing the evidence for precise costing and resourcing of operational activity, such as the London Olympic & Paralympic Games in 2012.

My experience as a senior detective in leading responses to critical incidents has been applied in the creation of transformational leadership events, such as 'Immerse', an award winning experiential learning and business simulation programme.

I have an ongoing interest in all forms of negotiation, persuasion and influence which stems from my experience as a hostage and crisis negotiator. I am also developing my expertise in the areas of risk and crisis management through the pursuit of knowledge concerning critical decision making, especially in conditions of uncertainty.

Experience

Director

Risk Angels Ltd
2007 – Present (8 years)London, United Kingdom

We're a London based business delivering risk and crisis management training, consultancy and expertise to the private, public and non-profit sectors. Using innovation, creativity and experience we develop people, teams and resilience in organisations.

To accomplish this goal we are able to draw upon a substantial cadre of trainers and consultants, but we only employ personnel with task relevant qualifications and operational experience, drawn primarily from United Kingdom law enforcement agencies, military, legal and academic institutions. The valuable experience, skills, knowledge and capability they bring to our business has enabled us to become a trusted consultancy to an increasingly diverse and international client base.

We also provide expertise to other consulting companies, 'white labelling' our delivery. We have developed significant experience in the oil and gas sector, providing risk and crisis management training, reviewing incident and emergency response arrangements and developing crisis management plans. We create realistic testing and exercising programmes to practice the response to critical events in the operational context of the client organisation. For this, we have created desktop scenarios, live exercises and immersive learning programmes.

Detective Chief Superintendent, London 2012 Olympic & Paralympic Games

Metropolitan Police

I led the planning at national level for serious & organised crime operations for the games. Hostage & Crisis Negotiator

Metropolitan Police
2003 – 2012 (9 years)New Scotland Yard

I became a nationally accredited hostage and crisis negotiator and was mainly deployed as a kidnap negotiator, providing a fast time response to kidnap for ransom. This role provided significant operational and strategic exposure to crisis management where lives were at risk. The coordinated efforts of specialised resources to address the tasks of communication, decision making in uncertainty and accountability for actions taken was an incredible experience that I was privileged to be part of over several years as a senior officer in an operational role. I was also part of cadre of trained negotiators responding to individuals in crisis and helping to dissuade them from taking their own lives.

This experience motivated further academic study on the subject of hostage taking for my M.Sc, resulting in a dissertation exploring the fate of victims of hostage events in Iraq & Afghanistan in the period after 2004.

Head of Covert Operations (Specialist Crime)

Metropolitan Police
2002 – 2010 (8 years)New Scotland Yard

I had the privilege of leading a team of highly skilled experts in infiltration techniques in order to resolve some of the most complex serious and organised crime cases and bring offenders to justice. As a transformational leader I provided the energy for change and modernisation, bringing in new practices which built on the long productive history of the department.

I created the covert policing ethics committee in 2008 to provide an additional layer of governance in the use of intrusive methods of investigation. I recruited academics, lawyers and senior people from NGOs to provide a sounding board for proposed operations, taking external views and feedback on the proportionality of covert policing methods.

I led the national training & development working group, modernising the selection, recruitment, training and continuing professional development of specialist officers. This was a significant project, negotiating with and influencing representatives from all UK police forces and other law enforcement agencies to accept and adopt proposals for radical reforms and modernisation, now embedded as 'Authorised Professional Practice' within the College of Policing.

I was elected as the chair of an international working group for two years in 2009, leading a network of colleagues sharing in good practice and specialist covert investigative techniques in more than 40 countries.

Commended for leadership of the unit by Commander, Covert Policing, New Scotland Yard in 2010.
