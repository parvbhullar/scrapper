Background

Summary

The Bomber Girls LRC is an ALL female motorcycle organization, formed in 2007. Our dedication to proudly support our military personnel and their families is well grounded and through shipping care packages to our Heroes, is 'world famous.' To us Patriotism is not just July 4th and Veterans Day. Patriotism is welcoming home and sending off deployed soldiers, writing them, and sending them pieces of home through care packages. Our fundraisers are not exactly "normal" but they are FUN and they raise money to send our soldiers what they need to know.. there are people at home in the U.S. that care about each and every one of them. WE SUPPORT OUR MILITARY by raising money with various motorcycle events to send care packages overseas!
We currently have chapters in:
Savannah, GA - Contact BomberGirlsLRC@gmail.com
Senoia, GA - Contact BomberGirlsLRCSenoia@gmail.com
Valparaiso, IN - Contact BomberGirlsLRCValpo@gmail.com
Support our Troops, Support their Families, our Heroes are important to us!

SPC Hilda Clayton Dignified Transfer(Link)

July 2013

aturday night, around 11, I received the email inviting the Bomber Girls to participate in a Dignified Transfer for SPC Hilda Clayton in Augusta. As the correspondance explained, a dignified transfer is escorting the fallen hero from the angel flight to the funeral home. Even though it has been raining like Noah moved next door, I knew this was something I had to do. So at o`dark thirty the next morning, allowing myself travel time as well as wait-the-rain-out time, I loaded up the bike and started my 3 hour journey to the Augusta Regional Airport.

The weather was mostly kind, and i arrived in plenty of time to meet Jerzey and the CVMA. 40 or so motorcycles showed up, which is quite an impressive sight. As our safety briefing and instructions came to a close, Pointman spoke about the SPC. he really brought this young, courageious, driven female soldier to life for all of us.

We stood at the tarmac fence as the plane arrived. Since I have began walking down this path of military support, I have attended many memorials, funeral processions and dedications. None of that prepared me for the moment when the flag draped coffin came into sight. And as Pointman said from behind me "up to this point, the family has only been told they lost their loved one. This is the moment they first see her, the moment they KNOW she is gone.", the raw emotion in the air was almost touchable. I could feel all of the veterans surrounding the area come to attention and salute as the honor guard brought SPC Clayton to her loved ones, gently placing her in the hearse. As we all walked to the bikes, I could see each one walking a little slower, head hanging a little lower. Tears rolled down my face for this young lady I didn't even know, for each face everyone of those veterans and civilians were remembering.

2013 Cookie Drive and Packing Party(Link)

January 2013

The ABATE Frosty Balls Run... wow, what a turnout!! I believe I heard 300+ registered riders.. right on! This was our 5th year piggybacking with ABATE on this run for the Cookie Drive... and it was an OVERWHELMING success again! A big Thank you to ABATE for that, and to Jr. ABATE for again holding their bake sale for people who didn't have cookies.

We were surprised by Walter Hicks, a HOG member who was on our mailing list this past year for care packages, with a touching speech and presentation of a cased flag and certificate. Thank you Walter, and welcome home.

And I know this is what you've all been waiting for..... final cookie numbers!

The grand total is.... ::drum roll:: ...... 5,885 DOZEN COOKIES!!! NO, that isn't a typo: Five Thousand Eight Hundred and Eighty Five. AND 99% were homemade this year! Wow, we're STILL in shock.

The US Military Vets MC Savannah Chapter still holds the local trophy for the most home baked cookies donated. 2,764 DOZEN cookies! The ENTIRE bed of a fullsize pickup truck was full of cookies. AMAZING.

Now, for those of you who are unfamiliar with us, and/or this particular event... we are a small local Savannah group, only a handful of patched members. However, we know people everywhere. Brenda Coleman, or Budgirl as we affectionately refer to her, once again organized her VFWs in and around Valparaiso and shipped down 1,619 DOZEN home baked cookies, clinching the title for the traveling trophy once again.

This morning at 7AM, the ladies drove the trailer over to the USPS and by late lunch, 194 boxes were processed and a $2,900.00 postage bill was paid. So keep an eye out guys and gals... cookies are on their way!!
