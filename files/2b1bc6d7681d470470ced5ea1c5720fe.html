Fast Recovery From Single or Dual Link Failure

February 2011 – June 2011

This project develops a novel mechanism for recovering from dual-link failures in IP networks. The packets are initially routed through the default link. On failure of this link, the packets are re-routed to the next link. The proposed technique requires three protection addresses for every node, in addition to the normal address. A node failure is assumed when three separate links connected to the same node are unavailable. The packet is then forwarded along a path to the destination avoiding the failed node.
The three servers are configured much before the client attempts a connection. It is also necessary that the three servers are synchronized so that the information provided to the client is the same irrespective of which server is communicating with the client. Thus, this pre-configuration of the servers results in fast and instantaneous recovery from not just one, but two failures.
