Background

Summary

I aim to educate, develop public support, end the stigma, and facilitate successful treatments for the leading cause of cancer deaths worldwide: lung cancer.

Anyone with lungs can get lung cancer. I've never smoked anything (except a salmon), yet I have metastatic lung cancer. I'm kept alive by clinical trials. I now use my science geek and communication skills to help others who have lung cancer, and increase the awareness of lung cancer among everyone else.

In my previous career in aerospace systems engineering, I was a “technical translator” of sorts: I researched science and technology developments and helped others understand how these developments could benefit them. Now I do the same for the experience and science of lung cancer. My Gray Connections blog has been named among the top lung cancer blogs and top cancer blogs.

My days are spent tracking developments in lung cancer research and treatments; supporting and informing lung cancer patients and caregivers online and in person; raising lung cancer awareness through writing, blogging and public speaking; collaborating with other patients, caregivers, advocates, healthcare providers, and researchers via the Lung Cancer Social Media (#LCSM) community as a moderator of #LCSM Chat; and promoting ePatient self-advocacy.

I intend to keep advocating for lung cancer patients until I'm out of treatment options and energy.
